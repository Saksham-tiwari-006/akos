Trademark Registration

Protect your brand identity with our expert trademark registration services.

What is Trademark Registration?

Trademark registration is the process of legally protecting a brand name, logo, slogan, or any unique sign that identifies your products or services. In simple words – it’s like giving your brand a legal shield so that no one else can copy or misuse your name, logo, or symbol. Once registered, you get the exclusive right to use it, and you can take legal action against anyone who tries to use it without permission.

According to the World Intellectual Property Organisation (WIPO), India has over 2.8 million active trademarks, with nearly 200,000 new applications filed each year. This sharp rise shows that more and more businesses in India are recognizing the importance of protecting their brand identity in today’s competitive and expanding economy.

Benefits of Trademark Registration

Registering your trademark offers several key advantages for your business:

  • Exclusive Rights: It gives you the exclusive right to use the mark for the goods and services you've registered.
  • Legal Protection: You can sue for infringement if someone else uses your mark without permission, giving you a strong legal standing.
  • Brand Recognition: A registered trademark (using the ® symbol) builds trust and goodwill among customers, enhancing your brand's reputation.
  • Asset Creation: A trademark is an intangible asset for your company. It can be sold, licensed, or franchised, adding value to your business.
  • Nationwide Protection: A single registration in India provides protection across the entire country.

What Can Be Trademarked?

A wide range of brand elements can be registered as a trademark, as long as they are unique and capable of distinguishing your goods or services from others. This includes:

  • Names: This can be a personal name, a company name, or a product name (e.g., TATA).
  • Logos and Symbols: Unique graphics or symbols that represent your brand (e.g., the Nike "swoosh").
  • Taglines or Slogans: Catchy phrases that are associated with your brand (e.g., "Just Do It").
  • Letters and Numerals: Specific combinations of letters or numbers.
  • Sound Marks: Distinctive sounds, like a jingle (e.g., the Intel inside sound).
  • Colour Combinations: Unique combinations of colors used in packaging or branding.

Who Can Apply For a Trademark in India?

In India, any person or business that owns a unique brand name, logo, or mark can apply for a trademark – even if the brand is not yet in use (you can file with “proposed to be used” status).
